# Tutors Charter

*This charter is not meant to create pressure, but to provide a common framework and expectations for everyone.*

## What are Tutors?

Tutors are students who help the school, make it alive and active, organise exams and coordinate various educational events at 42 Belgium. The tutoring system is based on volunteering. There is little to no mandatory involvement and everyone contributes at their own rhythm.

They help with:

- Exams
- Sprints
- She Loves To Code (SLTC)
- Workshops
- Community events
- Peer-to-peer support
- Any initiative helping the school community

Tutors are also contact people for students, especially for newcomers.

Tutors have their own website that you can consult and where you can apply: [tutors.42belgium.be](https://tutors.42belgium.be/)

## What is the Purpose of Tutoring?

The main goals of tutors are to:

- Encourage student involvement
- Strengthen peer-to-peer learning
- Support the school during educational events

To achieve those goals tutors are encouraged to:

- Take initiative
- Propose new ideas
- Organise events, activities, workshops, community initiatives
- Improve the school environment
- Help newer students integrate into the school

## What is Expected from Tutors?

Tutoring is a volunteer-based involvement. Tutors contribute when they can, how they can, and in the ways they want.

However, tutors are expected to:

- Respect school rules and procedures
- Help maintain a safe and fair environment
- Be respectful towards students and staff
- Communicate clearly with other tutors
- Participate seriously during exams or events they take part in
- Avoid any behavior that could damage trust in the tutoring system

Please note that if not enough tutors are available for an event or exam the activity may be canceled.

## Who Can Become a Tutor and How?

Anyone can become a tutor.

To become a tutor, you need to:

- Apply on the tutors website ([https://tutors.42belgium.be/](https://tutors.42belgium.be/))
- Attend a Tutor Kick-Off session

### Tutor Kick-Off Sessions

Kick-Off sessions are organised regularly:

- When new students start the cursus
- Once in the middle of a term

So approximately every three months.

The purpose of these sessions is to:

- Present the tutor charter
- Explain tutor responsibilities
- Present exam rules
- Answer questions

## General Organisation of Tutors

Tutors remain independent as long as they comply with school rules. Tutors are supervised by Supervising Tutors.

There are very few constraints unless tutors themselves decide to change the organisation.

Communication between tutors happens mainly through:

- Slack channels
- Peer to peer communication relayed by Supervising Tutors
- Internal organisation tools such as the tutors' platform [tutors.42belgium.be](http://tutors.42belgium.be) for exam organisation

## Exam Surveillance Charter

### General Principles

Exams must remain:

- Fair
- Calm
- Respectful
- Safe
- Equal for everyone

Every tutor supervising an exam agrees to apply and respect the following rules.

### Tutor Conduct During Exams

Tutors supervising exams must:

- Remain attentive during the entire supervision
- Keep the usage of their phone to the minimum
- Stay professional and respectful
- Check that students don't bring any forbidden items (phones, headphones, smartwatches, …)

Tutors should avoid:

- Looking directly at students' screens unnecessarily
- Creating stress or pressure
- Distracting students
- Discussing unrelated topics with other tutors during the exam

Tutors may:

- Answer organisational questions

Tutors must not:

- Answer pedagogical questions related to solving the exam
- Sleep during supervision
- Help cheating directly or indirectly
- Wear headphones or earphones on both ears

At least two tutors must remain in the cluster during exams. One supervising tutor should ALWAYS be supervising an exam session.

If suspicious behavior occurs:

- Take the student login
- Contact the bocal or supervising tutors
- Report the situation immediately

Any special case authorised by the bocal (early departure, medical issue, authorized phone access, etc.) will be indicated and reported to the supervising tutors before the exam.

### Seating Procedures

- Keep the 4 seats closest the entry for exceptions
- Fill first seats that are oriented towards the bocal then the other way
- Fill the seats in columns not rows

### Bathroom Procedures

Bathroom breaks are organised:

- Every 30 minutes
- With at least one tutor and one supervising tutor or bocal member accompanying students

### Exam Environment

Tutors are also responsible for:

- Managing window opening depending on noise or temperature
- Limiting disruptive behavior (keyboard smashing, loud noises, etc.)

### Student Rules During Exams

Students must:

- Show their badge at the beginning of the exam
- Stand up when in need to ask a question
- Respect silence
- Respect tutors instructions

Students can bring:

- 3 blank sheets of paper and a pen
- A bottle of water

Students may not:

- Leave during the first hour of the exam (except in case of success) or 2h42 for Phoenixes
- Use unauthorised devices
- Disturb other participants
- Wear caps or hoods
- Bring any connected devices (phones, smartwatches, headphones, etc.)

Standard campus rules such as no eating, no drinks other than water in enclosed bottles allowed or no bottles on the desk also apply during exams.

Students suspected of cheating must be reported immediately.

### Draft Papers

At the start of the exam, blank sheets of paper will be given by the bocal so that the tutors can distribute them if asked for by a student.

At the end of the exam, all draft papers, both used and unused, must be collected and returned to the bocal. They MUST NOT be thrown away.

### Benefits

Tutors who watch an exam receive:

- A fast pass (see section below)
- Logtime and wallets
- One drink per shift

### Fast Pass Rules

A Fast Pass is given to tutors after watching a student exam. A fast pass is the benefit of having priority to subscribe to an exam.

- Fast passes are only valid for the next two exam dates (in the student main campus)
- Fast passes are not given for piscine exams surveillance

## As a Tutor, What to Do If…

### I Want to Become a Supervising Tutor?

Contact a Supervising Tutor.

A discussion will be organised to:

- Understand your motivations
- Discuss your goals
- Explain the responsibilities
- Ensure good coordination and understanding within the supervising tutors team

### I Want to Leave the Tutors?

You are free to leave whenever you want.

Usually, leaving the tutor system simply means:

- Leaving the tutor communication channels
- Informing the supervising tutors if relevant

Leaving is never definitive. You can come back and become a tutor again later.
